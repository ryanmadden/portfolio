About

In April of 2014 I teamed up with three of my fraternity brothers to enter a 24-hour hackathon with the objective of creating a useful tool for students at Northwestern. Together we created Census, a web app that allows students to provide real-time feedback to their professors in large lectures. After a week of voting by the Northwestern community, Census took home the Student Choice Award!

My main focus during the development of Census was the design of the user interface and the JavaScript animation involved in the front-end.

When we set out to create Census, our goal was to improve the experience of Northwestern students in large lecture classes. Once a student selects their current class from a list, they are presented with the ‘Feedback’ screen. From there they can use the four button options to instantly send feedback to their lecturer’s screen. There is also the option to type in custom questions which are aggregated on the Professor’s interface.
